# YouTube Scriptwriting Frameworks and Principles

## Core Principles
1. **Narrative First**: Narrative > Immersion > Fluidity > Retention > Depth > Clarity.
2. **Seamless Transitions**: Avoid explicit signals like "In the next chapter...". Use consequence, contrast, or evolution of thought.
3. **Engagement Psychology**: Balance curiosity, narrative, revelation, and reflection.

## Introduction Frameworks
### 3A Framework (Attention → Authority → Agenda)
- **0–5s (Attention)**: Pattern interrupt, curiosity, contrast, or provocative question.
- **5–10s (Authority)**: Establish why the viewer should listen to you.
- **10–15s (Agenda)**: Clearly present the reward/value.

### Carrot with Chocolate
- **Promise (Carrot)**: Focus on what they *want* (the click-worthy desire).
- **Delivery (Chocolate)**: Provide what they *need* (the knowledge/transformation) alongside the desire.

## Retention Engineering
- **Open Loops**: Start a question/story and delay the answer.
- **Mini Cliffhangers**: End segments with a hook for the next.
- **Micro-rewards**: Frequent small payoffs for staying.
- **Interactive Directness**: Transform statements into questions or challenges to the viewer.

## Structural Standards
- **Word Count**: 130–150 words per minute of narration.
- **Chapter Length**: Minimum ~2 minutes of narration per chapter (~300 words).
- **Output Format**: Once approved, display only "Chapter Name" and "Script Text". No technical meta-data.
